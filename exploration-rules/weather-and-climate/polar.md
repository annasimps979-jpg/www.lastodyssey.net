# Polar

The farthest reaches of the poles and glaciers and cold regions during the winter resemble the Polar climate.  It is also useful for representing the peak of a tall mountain range.

**Example [Natural Hazards](/exploration-rules/hazards-and-encounters#term-natural-hazard):** fragile ice, a slippery slope, a deep snowbank.

## Weather Table

|Result|Weather|
|:-|:-|
|1-3|[Bright](/exploration-rules/weather-and-climate#term-bright)|
|4-7|[Snowy](/exploration-rules/weather-and-climate#term-snowy)|
|8-10|[Snowstorm](/exploration-rules/weather-and-climate#term-snowstorm)|

Due to the cold, [player characters](/extras/key-terms#term-player-character) in Polar regions should always be wearing a Coldproof [Cloak](/the-economy/items/travel-items#term-cloak) or piece of [armor](/the-economy/equipment/armor).  [Players](/extras/key-terms#term-player) whose [characters](/extras/key-terms#term-character) are not should roll the [outcome die](/extras/key-terms#term-outcome-die) during each [Camping Phase](/exploration-rules/the-travel-round#term-camping-phase) regardless of the weather.  Each character whose player rolls an 8 or above will develop the [Frostbitten](/combat-rules/statuses#term-frostbitten) [status](/combat-rules/statuses).  In Polar regions, the [navigation difficulty](/exploration-rules/nodes-and-paths#term-navigation-difficulty) of a path is 5 greater than it otherwise would be due to the effects of snow blindness, ice, snow, and the shifting of the ground.
