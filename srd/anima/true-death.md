[Heroes](/srd/glossary#term-hero) do not die like ordinary people.  Instead, they will only die under certain circumstances.  When this happens, they undergo something called _<dfn id = "term-true-death">True Death</dfn>._  If a Hero would undergo True Death due to the consequences of either their own actions or the actions of a [Villain](/srd/anima/antagonists/villains) or [Monster](/srd/anima/antagonists/monsters), they may spend 3 [<abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>](/srd/anima) to beseech their [eidolon](/srd/heroes/eidolons#term-eidolon) to save their life, in which case they will be revived at 1 [HP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-hp) either during the next round of combat or once they have made contact with their eidolon.  If they are unable to do this, they are gone forever, and cannot be resurrected by any means.  If they still have <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> remaining, it is distributed to the other Heroes by the dead Hero's player.  Any excess <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> that would bring any Hero above a total of 10 instead merely dissipates.  If there are no alive party members except for them, the <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> enters the [<abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> Pool](/srd/anima/spending#term-anima-pool).

There is no mechanical way to help another Hero avoid True Death, but [players](/srd/glossary#term-player) who would prefer their [characters](/srd/glossary#term-character) die instead of another's are free to ask the <abbr title="game master">[GM](/srd/glossary#term-gm)</abbr> to let this happen.  If the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> agrees, then their character suffers True Death instead of the intended target.  If a Hero undergoes True Death on behalf of another, they cannot revive themselves using <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>.

## Memorial Scenes

Character death in Last Odyssey is designed to be a rare and emotionally impactful event, and the group may need to take some time to process it, even if it was preplanned.  If appropriate, instead of immediately distributing the dead Hero's <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> to the remaining party members, the group may instead do so during a special [Scene](/srd/anima/scenes) called a _<dfn id = "term-memorial">Memorial.</dfn>_  Unlike other Scenes, Memorials have no central question.  Instead, each player states how their Hero responds to the loss of their party member, and then shares their favorite memory of the dead Hero.  The <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> also shares their favorite memory, and then the dead Hero's <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> is distributed among the living members of their party by the dead Hero's player.

## A Warrior's Death

[Antagonists](/srd/anima/antagonists) do not suffer True Death when they are [downed,](/srd/combat/injuries#term-downed) but the party may choose to kill them after the battle is over if they wish.  In contrast, [warriors,](/srd/warriors) whether on the side of the party or not, are much more fragile.  When a warrior is downed by lethal damage, the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> should roll the outcome die against a number of their choice to determine if they die.  The target number for this is up to the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr>, but in general fragile warriors will need a 3 or below, average warriors will need a 5 or below, and very tough warriors will need an 8 or even a 9 or below in order to survive.
