One of the two forms of Antagonist in Last Odyssey is the Monster.  Every Monster has a _<dfn id = "term-domain">Domain</dfn>,_ which is a territory that it defends and attempts to expand in order to earn [<abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>](/srd/anima#term-anima).  Domains are usually physical territories, such as a network of temples or a forgotten crypt, but they could also be located inside of more abstract locations like a dream realm, a pocket dimension, or a person's psyche.  A Monster's Domain will reflect its nature in recognizable ways.  Forests might grow dark and foreboding, or become infested with insects.  A feudal domain might become miserable and oppressive, while a city or town might experience a sudden, inexplicable bout of plague.  Defeating a Monster is often the key to ridding the world of such ills.  When a Monster is thrown out of its Domain, it might become a [Villain](/srd/anima/antagonists/villains) if it has the capacity.  Otherwise, if it is not promptly destroyed, it will eventually take root in a new Domain and continue to spread its influence.

## How Monsters Accrue <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>

New Monsters created by the <abbr title="game master">[GM](/srd/glossary#term-gm)</abbr> begin with up to 10 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> taken from the [<abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> Pool](/srd/anima/spending#term-anima-pool).  During the [campaign](/srd/glossary#term-campaign), there are four ways that a Monster can earn more:
*  For each <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> that [players](/srd/glossary#term-player) spend while inside of a Monster's Domain, the Monster earns 1 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>.  This effect takes precedence over <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> earned by a Villain.
*  Each time the players perform an [action](/srd/glossary#term-action) to significantly expand a Monster's Domain, whether willingly or otherwise, the Monster gains 1 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>.
*  Every time the Monster significantly expands its Domain or acquires a new one, it gains 5 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>.
*  Every time a [Hero](/srd/glossary#term-hero) uses <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> during [combat](/srd/combat) against a Monster, the Monster earns the same amount of <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> the Hero spent.

Monsters may also steal <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> from players by using the _<dfn id = "paradigm-devour">Devour</dfn>_ paradigm action.  During the [Paradigm Phase](/srd/combat/round#term-paradigm-phase), if at least one of the Monster's opponents is [downed](/srd/combat/injuries#term-downed), the Monster can attempt to grant them [True Death](/srd/anima/true-death).  When it does this, the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> casts the [outcome die](/srd/glossary#term-outcome-die) and then subtracts the amount of <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> the target has remaining from the result.  Then, the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> casts the outcome die again.  If this number is equal to or under the previously calculated result, the Hero undergoes True Death.  If not, they remain downed.  In either case, the Monster then gains the amount of <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> they absorbed from the Hero, and the [battle](/srd/glossary#term-battle) continues.

## Monster <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> Powers

Monsters use the power of <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> to protect themselves and to neutralize any threats to their Domain.  They can spend <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> to perform any of the following actions:

|Name|Cost|Description|
|:-|:-|:-|
|Interrupt|1 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>|A Monster may take one additional action during, before, or after another [character's](/srd/glossary#term-character) [turn](/srd/combat/round#term-turn).|
|Intervene|3 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>|When the narrative or mechanics indicate that a Hero would succeed at doing something inside of a Monster's Domain, such as after a [skill challenge](/srd/discovery/skill-challenges), the Monster may make them fail instead.|
|Revive|5 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>|If a Monster is downed during the Paradigm Phase, it may revive itself to full [HP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-hp) and [MP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-mp).  If it cannot do this, it will either move to its next Form, Retreat, or die.|
|Grow|10 <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>|A Monster may spend <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> to acquire a new Form.  The <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> should create this Form using the same process for creating the Monster's initial Forms.|

## Monster Capabilities

While inside of its Domain, a Monster has a lot of latitude to interfere with the players' progress.  <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr>s who want to increase the horror factor can make Monsters behave in unpredictable ways.  Here are some possible actions Monsters may take within their Domain without spending <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr>:

|Name|Description|
|:-|:-|
|Influence|Even when not physically present, Monsters can remind players that they are in their Domain.  Bugs will crawl in strange patterns, mysterious howling and barking can be heard at night, spectral forms will invade people's dreams, or anything else the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> can think of.  More powerful monsters might even be able to inflict real injuries or scare threats into leaving.|
|Storm|Monsters can distort the behavior of nature within their Domains to their own ends, and that includes the weather.  While the players are traveling within a Monster's Domain, the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> should sometimes intervene during the [travel round](/srd/exploration/travel/round) to make the weather more dangerous than what the [weather table](/srd/exploration/weather#term-weather-table) would indicate.  Characters who use the [Divine](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-divine-action) action will still be able to predict the weather if this happens, but will notice that something is amiss.|
|Spawn|Monsters have [warrior](/srd/warriors#term-warrior) underlings such as pack animals, evil spirits, and bands of thugs that they will periodically create more of to further cement their power in a Domain.  The presence of these types of warriors and the players' inability to get rid of them is a clear sign that a Monster is nearby.|
|Ambush|A deep understanding, whether instinctive or intellectual, of a Monster's domain allows them to navigate it much more easily than the players can.  The Monster is always able to [ambush](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-ambush) the Heroes while they are in their domain, either as a plot event or in place of a warrior encounter during the travel round.|

## Monster Creation

To create a new Monster for their campaign, the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> should take the following steps:
1.  Create a concept.  What is the Monster's appearance?  How does it behave?  Does it have any desires, or is it acting on pure instinct?  Every Monster also begins with an amount of <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> from the <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> Pool.
2.  Determine its Domain.  The easiest way to do this is to designate one or more [paths](/srd/exploration/maps#term-path) and/or [nodes](/srd/exploration/maps#term-node) where the Monster is active.  A Monster's Domain can be as large or fragmented as the <abbr title="game master">GM</abbr> wants, although they should keep in mind that the larger a Monster's Domain, the more <abbr title = "anima">🜉</abbr> it will be able to make use of.
3.  Choose the [creature type](/srd/warriors/types#term-creature-type), [element](/srd/combat/elements#term-element), appearance, and [tier](/srd/heroes/creation#term-tier) of each of the Monster's Forms.
4.  Calculate the [statistics](/srd/glossary#term-statistic) and [abilities](/srd/glossary#term-ability) for each of the Monster's Forms, including any paradigm actions.  The rules for this can be found in the [Warrior Creation](/srd/warriors/creation) subsection of the [Warriors](/srd/warriors) section.
5.  Create a few [Normal](/srd/warriors/creation#normal-warrior) and [Elite](/srd/warriors/creation#elite-warrior) warriors to serve as the Monster's underlings.  Does the Monster have a brood of tiny versions of itself?  Are they the leader of a [faction](/srd/intro/advice/worldbuilding#term-faction), or do they influence their minions in more subtle ways?
