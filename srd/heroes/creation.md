# Hero Creation

In Last Odyssey, each [player](/srd/key-terms#term-player) plays the part of a single [character](/srd/key-terms#term-character) known as a [Hero](/srd/key-terms#term-hero).  Heroes will travel the world, taking on [quests](/srd/discovery/quests), defeating [enemies](/srd/enemies), and being a force for positive change.  As they advance through the [campaign](/srd/key-terms#term-campaign), they will gain new [abilities](/srd/key-terms#term-ability) and increase their [attributes and statistics](/srd/heroes/numbers).  The basic measure of a character's strength is a number called their _<dfn id="term-tier">tier</dfn>,_ which is a number from 1 to 10 that acts as a rough indicator of how high the character's statistics and attributes will be, as well as how many abilities they have.  The following section outlines the process for making a new tier 1 Hero.  For Heroes beyond tier 1, see the [Hero advancement](/srd/heroes/advancement) section.

## Create Concept
The first thing players should do before writing down any numbers is think a little bit about what kind of people their Heroes are--their beliefs, their background, any quirks of appearance, and so on.  They will then write down a few sentences describing any details that immediately come to mind.  Once they've done this, there are two more parts to their Hero concepts that they should decide on before moving forward.  The first of these is a Hero's _<dfn id="term-aspiration">Aspiration</dfn>,_ which is what drives them--what they want to accomplish, what kind of person they want to be, or what code of honor or ethics they follow.  Example Aspirations include learning their identity, adhering to the code of knightly honor, or reconnecting with a family member.

The second of these is their _<dfn id="term-relationships">Relationships</dfn>,_ which are the emotions they feel towards the people or groups to which they are most strongly connected.  This could be anything from ethnic or religious affiliation to a Hero's family, their best friends, or their lovers.  Writing down Relationships with other [Heroes](/srd/key-terms#term-hero) is also strongly encouraged.  A Hero starts with one to three Relationships and one Aspiration.  Players will change their Heroes' Relationships and Aspirations as the story progresses.  A given Hero may only ever have one Aspiration at a time, although they can have as many Relationships as is appropriate.  After the players have each written a sentence or two describing a Hero's Aspiration and each of their Relationships, they have enough to count as a concept.  They should also begin to think about their Heroes' names and appearances.

In the event that a Hero lacks an Aspiration, their player may leave it blank.  In this case, they will fill it in at some point during the campaign.  For example, something might happen to them or a loved one that will cause them to swear revenge on the central [Villain](/srd/anima/antagonists/villains) of the story.  However, because Aspirations and Relationships are required for earning Anima, a Hero must have at least one of either before the creation process is over.  If a Hero begins the game without an Aspiration, they also may not summon their [eidolon](/srd/heroes/eidolons) or save themselves from [True Death](/srd/anima/true-death) until they do so.

## Set Attributes
After the players have made their concepts, the next step is to determine their Heroes' attributes.  These will also determine their starting statistics.  In Last Odyssey, each Hero has three of these:
*  A Hero's _[Body](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-body)_ contributes to their [Health](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-health), [Physical Accuracy](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-physical-accuracy), and [Status Accuracy](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-status-accuracy).
*  A Hero's _[Mind](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-mind)_ contributes to their [Initiative](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-initiative), [Magical Accuracy](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-magical-accuracy), and Physical Accuracy.
*  A Hero's _[Spirit](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-spirit)_ contributes to their [Mana](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-mana), Status Accuracy, and Magical Accuracy.

At Hero creation, players may either set the value of all of a Hero's attributes to 2, or set one of them to 3, another to 2, and the remaining attribute to 1.

## Choose Archetype
Next, the party will choose their Heroes' [archetypes](/srd/heroes/archetypes), which give them extra abilities and [talents](/srd/heroes/advancement#term-talent) that they can acquire as they level up, as well as bonuses to their statistics.  In Last Odyssey, there are nine archetypes in total:
*  _[Rangers](/srd/heroes/archetypes/ranger)_ are heroes of exceptional focus.
*  _[Thieves](/srd/heroes/archetypes/thief)_ are heroes of exceptional cunning.
*  _[Artificers](/srd/heroes/archetypes/artificer)_ are heroes of exceptional knowledge.
*  _[Sages](/srd/heroes/archetypes/sage)_ are heroes of exceptional wisdom.
*  _[Priests](/srd/heroes/archetypes/priest)_ are heroes of exceptional compassion.
*  _[Dancers](/srd/heroes/archetypes/dancer)_ are heroes of exceptional charm.
*  _[Knights](/srd/heroes/archetypes/knight)_ are heroes of exceptional discipline.
*  _[Commanders](/srd/heroes/archetypes/commander)_ are heroes of exceptional leadership.
*  _[Harlequins](/srd/heroes/archetypes/harlequin)_ are heroes of exceptional daring.

At Hero creation, a character's archetype determines the following traits:
*  Armor Proficiency: A Hero's _<dfn id="term-armor-proficiency">armor proficiency</dfn>_ is what type of [armor](/srd/economy/equipment/armor) they can wear.  At Hero creation, the Hero starts with a [Mundane](/srd/economy/equipment#term-equipment-quality) piece of that type of armor equipped.
*  Weapon Proficiency: A Hero's _<dfn id="term-weapon-proficiency">weapon proficiency</dfn>_ is what type of [weapon](/srd/economy/equipment/weapons) they can wield.  At Hero creation, the Hero starts with a Mundane version of that type of weapon equipped.  Make sure to note down what statistic the Hero's weapon gives them a bonus to in addition to [Physical Attack](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-strike) and [Magical Attack](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-spell).
*  [Buff](/srd/combat/buffs-and-debuffs#term-buff): Archetype sets the statistic that the Hero buffs when they play the [Support](/srd/heroes/roles/support) [role](/srd/heroes/roles).  Later on, they will also gain permanent bonuses to it as they advance in tier.
*  [Debuff](/srd/combat/buffs-and-debuffs#term-debuff): This sets the statistic that the Hero debuffs when they play the [Saboteur](/srd/heroes/roles/saboteur) role.
*  Stat Bonuses: A Hero's archetype grants them fixed bonuses to their Health, Initiative, and Mana.

A Hero's archetype also provides them with a set of abilities they can use regardless of what role they play, as well as a set of special [passive](/srd/combat/attacks#term-passive) abilities called talents that they will acquire as they advance in tier.  At Hero creation, they begin with the ability to use their archetype's rank 1 ability.

## Choose Accessory

A tier 1 Hero has one [accessory](/srd/economy/equipment/accessories#term-accessory) slot that their player may choose to fill with either a [Lucky](/srd/economy/equipment/accessories#term-lucky-accessory) accessory or a [Mystical](/srd/economy/equipment/accessories#term-mystical-accessory) accessory.  A Lucky accessory gives them a +1 bonus to their [Evasion](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-evasion), while a Mystical accessory gives them a +1 bonus to their [Resistance](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-resistance).  When choosing an accessory, choose its type, of which there are three:
* Head accessories are worn on the head, such as circlets and hoods.
* Loop accessories are worn on the chest and waist, and include both necklaces and magic belts.
* Arm accessories are worn on limbs, and include things like rings and armbands.

## Choose Element
A Hero's [element](/srd/combat/elements#term-element) determines the element of some of the abilities they gain from their roles.  More specifically, they determine the element of the damage they deal with certain [Striker](/srd/heroes/roles/striker) and [Caster](/srd/heroes/roles/caster) abilities, the element of the [Shells](/srd/combat/shells) they apply and the damage they can immunize their [party](/srd/key-terms#term-party) members from as a Support, and the [status](/srd/combat/statuses) they can apply as a Saboteur.  The nine elements are [Fire](/srd/combat/elements#term-fire), [Water](/srd/combat/elements#term-water), [Earth](/srd/combat/elements#term-earth), [Wind](/srd/combat/elements#term-wind), [Thunder](/srd/combat/elements#term-thunder), [Ice](/srd/combat/elements#term-ice), [Wood](/srd/combat/elements#term-wood), [Light](/srd/combat/elements#term-light), and [Shadow](/srd/combat/elements#term-shadow).

By default, Heroes count as [colorless](/srd/combat/elements#term-colorless) for the purposes of calculating the damage they take from attacks regardless of their element.  However, a Hero may instead have an _<dfn id = "term-innate-element">innate</dfn>_ element, which means that they also count as being of their element for the purpose of determining the results of attacks.  This choice can only be made at Hero creation, and is permanent.

## Choose Starting Role
A Hero's role grants them abilities they can use in [combat](/srd/combat), and extra actions they can take during [exploration](/srd/exploration).  It also grants them bonuses to three different statistics that depend on which role they currently play.  As they advance in tier, Heroes will gain multiple roles that they can switch between during the [Paradigm Phase](/srd/combat/round#term-paradigm-phase) of combat.  Eventually, every Hero in the party will be able to use any role.  However, at Hero creation all Heroes start with only one role available.  See the [Roles](/srd/heroes/roles) section for more details.  In Last Odyssey, there are six roles:
*  _[Strikers](/srd/heroes/roles/striker)_ specialize in dealing physical damage to their enemies.
*  _[Casters](/srd/heroes/roles/caster)_ specialize in dealing magical damage to their enemies.
*  _[Healers](/srd/heroes/roles/healer)_ specialize in restoring their allies' [HP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-hp) and negating debuffs and statuses.
*  _[Defenders](/srd/heroes/roles/defender)_ specialize in drawing enemy attacks and negating the damage done to themselves and their allies.
*  _[Supports](/srd/heroes/roles/support)_ specialize in buffing allies and applying special statuses called Shells, which act as a form of temporary HP.
*  _[Saboteurs](/srd/heroes/roles/saboteur)_ specialize in debuffing and applying negative statuses to enemies.

When the players choose their Heroes' starting role, they automatically gain access to that role's rank 1 ability, which they should mark down on their character sheets.

Each role grants extra bonuses to three of a Hero's statistics equal to each of their attributes that are listed in their description in the [Roles](/srd/heroes/roles) section.  Whenever a Hero takes a new role, including now, note down the statistics that they give bonuses to.  When calculating a Hero's statistics, these are taken into account.  Eventually, a Hero will have six separate sets of statistics, one for each role.

## Calculate Statistics
At this point, the players should now have enough information to determine their Heroes' statistics.  Below, the formulae for determining each of them are given in shorthand.  To see them in more detail and learn more about what each statistic does, see the [Numbers](/srd/heroes/numbers) section as well as the [Combat Rules](/srd/combat/combat).
*  _Health_ determines a Hero's maximum HP.  It is equal to (Body)x5 + (Archetype Bonus).
*  _Initiative_ determines when a Hero acts during combat.  It is equal to (Mind)x2 + (Archetype Bonus).
*  _Mana_ determines a Hero's maximum [MP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-mp).  It is equal to (Spirit)x5 + (Archetype Bonus).
*  _Physical Attack_ determines a Hero's [base](/srd/key-terms#term-base) physical damage bonus.  It is equal to (Weapon Bonus) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _Magical Attack_ determines a Hero's base magical damage bonus.  It is equal to (Weapon Bonus) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _[Physical Defense](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-physical-defense)_ determines the base reduction to the physical damage a Hero takes.  It is equal to (Armor Bonus) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _[Magical Defense](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-magical-defense)_ determines the base reduction to the magical damage a Hero takes.  It is equal to (Armor Bonus) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _Evasion_ determines the base reduction to enemies' chance to hit a Hero when they attack.  It is equal to (Accessory Bonus if applicable) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _Resistance_ determines the base reduction to enemies' chance to inflict a status on a Hero.  It is equal to (Accessory Bonus if applicable) + (Role Bonus if applicable).
*  _[Physical Accuracy](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-physical-accuracy)_ determines a Hero's bonus chance to hit with [strikes](/srd/combat/attacks#term-strike).  It is equal to (Mind) + (Body).
*  _[Magical Accuracy](/srd/heroes-numbers#term-magical-accuracy)_ determines a Hero's bonus chance to hit with [spells](/srd/combat/attacks#term-spell).  It is equal to (Spirit) + (Mind).
*  _[Status Accuracy](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-status-accuracy)_ determines a Hero's chance to hit with [sleights](/srd/combat/attacks#term-sleight).  It is equal to (Body) + (Spirit).
*  _[Carrying Capacity](/srd/economy/items#term-carrying-capacity)_ determines how many items a Hero can hold in their inventory at once.  It is equal to 10 + (Body)x5.

## Create Eidolon
At this stage in the process, the players should now be ready to create their Heroes' eidolons.  An eidolon is a being whose appearance in the world is the most powerful manifestation of a Hero's magic.  Think of them as a combination between a patron saint and a guardian angel.  Each Hero has a single eidolon that grows more powerful as they grow more powerful.  In combat, Heroes can spend [Anima](/srd/anima#term-anima) to summon their eidolons.

During Hero creation, record every Hero's eidolon's name, appearance,  and statistics.  Its statistics are determined by the formulae listed in the [Eidolons](/srd/heroes/eidolons) section.  The exception to this is that eidolons do not have Mana because their attacks are either free or have an Anima cost, and do not have Initiative because they act during the same turn as their summoner.  In addition, a Hero's eidolon has an innate element which is the same as their summoner's element.  This is true whether their summoner's element is innate or not.

Each eidolon has one of six possible [classes:](/srd/heroes/eidolons#term-class) Striker, Caster, Healer, Defender, Support, or Saboteur.  All eidolons can use the Attack and Full Restore abilities, and also gains two more abilities from its class.  See the Eidolons section for more details.
*  Striker eidolons gain the Cleave and Elemental Strike eidolon abilities.
*  Caster eidolons gain the Magic Bolt and Elemental Bolt eidolon abilities.
*  Healer eidolons gain the Heal and Cure eidolon abilities.
*  Defender eidolons gain the Shield and Barrier eidolon abilities.
*  Support eidolons gain the Enhance and Shell eidolon abilities.
*  Saboteur eidolons gain the Reduce and Sabotage eidolon abilities.

## Finishing Touches
At this point in Hero creation, players have all of the necessary information required to play their Heroes.  Make sure all of it is recorded properly on their character sheets.  In addition, be sure to write down any other relevant information, such as their height, weight, gender, eye color, backstory, real and apparent age, and other story elements.  In addition, give each Hero 5 starting Anima out of a maximum of 10.  They will earn more during [Scenes](/srd/anima/scenes#term-scene) over the course of the game.

Finally, all Heroes receive 10 cr at Hero creation.  Players may use some or all of this to purchase [battle items](/srd/economy/items/battle).  Whatever amount is left over after they are done purchasing items is carried with them into the beginning of the game.

Before each player is finished with Hero creation, they should check--have they:
1.  Given their Hero a name and appearance?
2.  Chosen their Hero's Aspiration and Relationships?
3.  Set their Hero's Body, Mind, and Spirit?
4.  Chosen their Hero's archetype, and recorded their rank 1 ability?
5.  Recorded their Hero's weapon proficiency and starting weapon?
6.  Recorded their Hero's armor proficiency and starting armor?
7.  Given their Hero either a Lucky or Mystical accessory?
8.  Recorded the buff and debuff associated with their Hero's archetype?
9.  Chosen their Hero's element?
10.  Chosen their Hero's starting role, and recorded its rank 1 ability?
11.  Recorded the three bonuses to their Hero's statistics that their starting role provides?
12.  Calculated their Hero's Health, Initiative, Mana, Physical Attack, Magical Attack, Physical Defense, Magical Defense, Evasion, Resistance, Physical Accuracy, Magical Accuracy, Status Accuracy, and Carrying Capacity correctly according to the provided formulae?
13.  Recorded all of their eidolon's statistics?
14.  Recorded their eidolon's element?
15.  Recorded their Eidolon's four abilities?
16.  Written down their Eidolon's name and appearance?
17.  Given their Hero 10 cr, and bought any items with it that they can afford?
18.  Given their Hero 5 starting Anima?

If you have done all of this, then congratulations, you are now ready to play Last Odyssey!

## Higher Tier Heroes

To create a new Hero for a pre-existing campaign or for a campaign that will begin at a tier greater than 1, add Hero advancements per the [Hero advancement](/srd/heroes/advancement) section one at a time until the Hero reaches the appropriate tier.  In addition, Heroes will also start with different equipment depending on their tier:
* Heroes of tier 4-7 will start with a Rare weapon and armor of the appropriate type, as well as two Lucky or Mystical accessories and 100 cr.
* Heroes of tier 8-10 will start with an Expert weapon and armor of the appropriate type, as well as three Lucky or Mystical accessories and 1000 cr.
