# Weather and Climate

A large contributing factor to the character of an [overworld](/srd/exploration#term-overworld) in Last Odyssey is its climate.  An overworld's _<dfn id = "term-climate">climate</dfn>_ determines the weather that the [players](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-player) will encounter as they traverse its paths.  For each different distinct climate the players encounter, there is a different corresponding _<dfn id = "term-weather-table">weather table</dfn>._  To make a roll on the weather table, the [GM](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-gm) should roll the [outcome die](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-outcome-die).  The number shown on the die determines the type of weather that the players will encounter for that day.  The exception to this is if a type of weather called a Storm took place the day before, in which case the GM should instead determine the weather based on the rules for the particular Storm instead.

In order of danger, the possible weather effects are shown in the table below.

|Weather|Effect|
|:-|:-|
|<dfn id = "term-sunny">Sunny</dfn>|The sky is a pleasant, clear blue, and the temperature is mild.  The players do not experience any penalties or bonuses.|
|<dfn id = "term-cloudy">Cloudy</dfn>|The sky is filled with clouds that cast shadows over the ground, and the temperature is cool.  The players do not experience any bonuses or penalties.|
|<dfn id = "term-bright">Bright</dfn>|The sun is out and beating down right on the [characters](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-character).  At the end of the [Camping Phase](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-camping-phase), all characters that did not wear a Sunproof [Cloak](/srd/economy/items/travel#term-cloak) or piece of [armor](/srd/economy/equipment/armor) will receive the [Burned](/srd/combat/statuses#term-burned) [status](/srd/combat/statuses).  During [combat](/srd/combat) [encounters](/srd/enemies/encounters#term-encounter), all characters who are not wearing a Sunproof Cloak will begin the first [round](/srd/combat/round#term-round) with the [Blinded](/srd/combat/statuses#term-blinded) status.|
|<dfn id = "term-rainy">Rainy</dfn>|Rain is coming down from the clouds, making tools and equipment damp.  All characters that did not wear a Waterproof Cloak or piece of armor will lose one [item](/srd/economy/items) of the GM's choice due to their pack filling with water.  On Rainy days, the [navigation difficulty](/srd/exploration/nodes-and-paths#term-navigation-difficulty) of the path counts as 1 greater than normal.|
|<dfn id = "term-foggy">Foggy</dfn>|A fine, white mist cloaks everything, making it significantly harder to see.  On Foggy days, the navigation difficulty of the path counts as 1 greater than normal.  In addition, enemies during enemy encounters will gain [First Strike](/srd/combat/starting#term-first-strike) unless the [Scout](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-scout-action) [action](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-action) was used the day before.|
|<dfn id = "term-snowy">Snowy</dfn>|Snow drifts gently down from the sky, piling up on the ground and creating problems for the unwary.  Players whose characters that did not wear a Coldproof Cloak or piece of armor should roll a 1d4 during the Camping Phase.  Each character whose player rolls a 4 will develop the [Frostbitten](/srd/combat/statuses#term-frostbitten) status.|
|<dfn id = "term-hot">Hot</dfn>|It is unbelievably hot, far hotter than the human body can truly handle.  At the end of the Camping Phase, every player whose character did not wear a Heatproof Cloak or piece of armor should roll a 1d4.  If they roll a 1, their character takes an [injury](/srd/combat/death-and-injury#term-injury) as though they had been [downed](/srd/combat/death-and-injury#term-downed) in [battle](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-battle).|
|<dfn id = "term-humid">Humid</dfn>|It should be raining, but it isn't, and it's so hot and sticky out that it's difficult to breathe.  At the end of the Camping Phase, all characters that did not wear a Waterproof Cloak will receive the [Poisoned](/srd/combat/statuses#term-poisoned) status.  Characters that do not wear a Heatproof Cloak should roll a 1d4.  If they roll a 1, their character takes an injury as though they had been downed in battle.|

_<dfn id = "term-storm">Storms</dfn>_ are special types of weather that last for multiple days if triggered.  During the [Navigation Phase](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-navigation-phase) the following day after the GM rolls a Storm on the weather table, the weather is not determined as normal.  Instead, the GM rolls a 1d4 and add 6 to determine the result on the weather table for that day.  There are three types of Storms: Thunderstorms, Snowstorms, and Sandstorms.

|Storm|Effect|
|:-|:-|
|<dfn id = "term-thunderstorm">Thunderstorm</dfn>|The sky opens up, rain pours down, and lightning begins to boom.  All characters that did not wear a Waterproof Cloak or piece of armor will lose 2 items of the GM's choice due to their pack filling with water.  Also, if no character takes the [Shelter](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-shelter-action) action during the Camping Phase, the characters will be unable to use the [Cook](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-cook-action), [Recover](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-recover-action), or [Mend](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-mend-action) actions, and will take an injury as though they had run out of [Supplies](/srd/economy/items/travel#term-supplies).  The navigation difficulty of a path counts as 2 greater than normal during a Thunderstorm.|
|<dfn id = "term-snowstorm">Snowstorm</dfn>|High winds blow swirling vortices of snow and sleet directly into the faces of the characters, obscuring the trail and making it difficult to camp.  During a Snowstorm, enemies during enemy encounters will gain First Strike unless the Scout action was used the day before.  At least two characters should take the Shelter action during the camping phase, or else characters will be unable to use the Cook, Recover, or Mend actions, and will take an injury as though they had run out of Supplies as well as gaining the Frostbitten status.  The navigation difficulty of a path counts as 2 greater than normal during a Snowstorm.  Finally, at the end of the Camping Phase, all characters that did not wear a Coldproof Cloak or piece of armor will receive the Frostbitten status regardless of whether they take the Shelter action during camping.|
|<dfn id = "term-sandstorm">Sandstorm</dfn>|During a Sandstorm, enemies during enemy encounters will gain First Strike unless the Scout action was used the day before.  At least two characters should take the Shelter action during the Camping Phase, or else characters will be unable to use the Cook, Recover, or Mend actions.  No matter what, they are also unable to use the Scout and [Watch](/srd/exploration/travel/round#term-watch-action) actions during the Camping Phase.  The navigation difficulty of a path counts as 3 greater than normal during a Sandstorm.|

GMs are encouraged to build their own weather tables for different areas out of the weather effects described above.  This section contains some example climate types that are ready to use in your [campaign](/srd/extras/key-terms#term-campaign):
*  [Coastal:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/coastal) Mild and warm.
*  [Temperate:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/temperate) Cool and wet.
*  [Boreal:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/boreal) Cold and wet.
*  [Tropical:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/tropical) Hot and wet.
*  [Desert:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/desert) Hot and dry.
*  [Polar:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/polar) Cold and dry.
*  [Climate Modifiers:](/srd/exploration/weather-and-climate/climate-modifiers) Extra effects for more fantastical weather types.

While players are not traveling, the weather does not have any mechanical effects.  However, to add flavor, GMs may choose to roll on the weather table every 24 in-game hours to determine the weather that day.  This can add to the character of a settlement and act as a way to inform players on the type of weather they might face during travel.

Sometimes, the players will spend enough time in an overworld that the seasons will change around them.  If this is the case, the GM can opt to keep track of the days as they go by with a seasonal calendar.  Every time the players complete a travel round while traveling or rest for the day at a specific location, the GM should advance the day on the calendar by 1.  If they feel like it, GMs can get creative with this, choosing festival days, marking out special seasons like wildfires or high winds, and creating transitional periods when one season becomes another.  Doing so will give Last Odyssey a more pastoral tone, and GMs and players should discuss whether or not this is the type of game they'd like to play.
