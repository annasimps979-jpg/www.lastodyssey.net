# Shells

Shell [abilities](/srd/key-terms#term-ability) apply a special [status](/srd/combat/statuses) to their target known as a _<dfn id = "term-shell">Shell</dfn>._  Shells do not work the same as other statuses, since they cannot be removed by abilities that normally remove statuses and will also persist outside of combat, although they will reset to 0 whenever characters [rest](/srd/exploration/resting).  A Shell is a pool of temporary [HP](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-hp) that has a fixed [element](/srd/combat/elements#term-element).  When a [character](/srd/key-terms#term-character) has a Shell applied to them, they will take double damage from all [spells](/srd/combat/attacks#term-spell).  In addition, when determining the elemental multiplier to the damage they take or to their status modifier,  take the target's element to be the element of the Shell.  No character may have more than one Shell of a given element active at a time, and no [Hero](/srd/key-terms#term-hero) may ever have more Shell than their [base](/srd/key-terms#term-base) [Health](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-health).  [Enemies](/srd/enemies) also begin [battle](/srd/key-terms#term-battle) with their maximum Shell already applied.  An enemy's maximum Shell is determined by the starting Shell they have in [combat](/srd/combat), and they cannot receive a Shell higher than this of any element.

When a character's Shell is reduced to zero, they are referred to as _<dfn id = "term-shellbroken">Shellbroken</dfn>._  When a character's Shell is first broken by an attack, subtract the amount of damage the attack did to the character's Shell from the total final damage of the attack, modifiers included.  If the resulting number is greater than the character's remaining HP, the character is [downed](/srd/heroes/numbers#term-downed).  If the resulting number is less than the character's remaining HP, they do not take any additional damage.
